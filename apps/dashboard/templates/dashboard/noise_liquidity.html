Noise as Information for Illiquidity

"The level of liquidity in the aggregate financial market is closely connected to the amount of arbitrage capital available. Thus, temporary price deviations, or noise in prices, being a key symptom of shortage in arbitrage capital, contains important information about the amount of liquidity in the aggregate market."
